"Named patient supply," also referred to as "named patient program," or "compassionate use," is a provision in the pharmaceutical industry that allows patients to gain access to drugs that are not yet approved in their country. Regulatory authorities in various countries oversee these programs to ensure they are conducted ethically and safely, balancing the need for access to potentially life-saving drugs with the requirement to protect patients from unproven or unsafe treatments

Circumstances under which Named patient supply is used

1

For Drugs in Development: If a drug is still in the clinical trial phase or under review for market approval, but preliminary data suggests it could be beneficial for certain conditions, doctors may be able to request it on behalf of their patients. This is particularly common for patients who have exhausted all other treatment options and cannot wait for the drug to be commercially available.

2

For Rare Diseases: In the case of rare diseases, where drug development is limited and might not be commercially viable for widespread distribution, manufacturers may provide the drug on a named patient basis to meet the needs of these patients.

3

For Drugs Not Available in the Patient's Country: Even if a drug is approved and available in some parts of the world, regulatory and commercial reasons might delay or prevent its availability in other regions. Named patient programs can facilitate access to these medications for patients in countries where the drug is not yet approved or marketed.

Setps involved in named patient supply

1

Once availability of medicine is confirmed, payment to be processed from your end.

2

Once the product is ready, the origin export formalities will take 2-3 days.

3

In the meantime you can apply for import/licenses for import clearance.

4

Transit time will be minimum 3-4 days.

5

Upon package arrival in your country, clearance will take minimum 5-6 days.

6

Transit time will be minimum 3-4 days.

Document required for this process

  • Patient ID
  • Valid Dr. Prescription
  • Import Permit (Form 12B) link
  • Customs Duty Exemption Certificate(CDEC) [Applicable only for Life Saving drugs]
  • Duty Bond (If requested by Customs)
  • Authorization Letter (CHA/Forwarder)
  • Delivery Order (DO) Collection Letter
